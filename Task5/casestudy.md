
<h1>Ransomware Case Study</h1>

<p>
Ransomware is a type of cyberattack where hackers lock your computer files and ask for money to unlock them. 
In this fictional case study, we see how this can seriously affect students and colleges.
</p>

<p>
A student named Riya received an email that looked like it came from her university’s IT department. 
The email said she must “update her antivirus software immediately” and gave her a link to download the update. 
Thinking it was real, Riya clicked the link and installed the file. Within seconds, her laptop froze. 
All her important documents—project work, notes, photos, and assignments—became locked. 
A message appeared asking her to pay ₹25,000 in cryptocurrency within 48 hours to get her files back.
</p>

<p>This attack followed the common ransomware pattern:</p>
<ul>
    <li>The hacker sends a fake email.</li>
    <li>The victim clicks the dangerous link.</li>
    <li>The ransomware installs and encrypts all files.</li>
    <li>A ransom message appears asking for money.</li>
    <li>The hacker threatens to delete or leak the data if payment is not made.</li>
</ul>

<p>
Students, small companies, and people with weak security are usually targeted because they are easier victims. 
Riya became very stressed and worried about losing her final project and her personal files being leaked.
</p>

<p>
The results were serious: loss of data, mental stress, possible money loss, and the need to reset her entire laptop. 
This story shows how one simple click can cause a huge problem, and why we should always double-check emails and avoid unknown links.
</p>
